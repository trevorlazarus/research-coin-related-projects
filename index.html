A journal is a club: A new economic model for scholarly publishing

  1. Propose a new economic model for analysis of scholarly publishing—journal publishing in particular—is proposed that draws on club theory
  2. Public good (new scholarly knowledge)
  3. In the older model, publishing is communication, as the dissemination of information.
  4. A club model views publishing as a group formation
  5. A new economic model of a journal as a ‘knowledge club’ is elaborated
  6. If a journal is a club, we should be creating new types of journals

A Proposed Currency System for Academic Peer Review Payments Using the BlockChain Technology

  1. The currency(r-coin) they propose could be used as an alternative publication metric.
  2. This provides a greater incentive to carry out peer review and raises the quality of those reviews.
  3. Also propose a mechanism to track the review process that has the potential for wider application within the academic publishing environment as an alternative metric to those used at present.

A Proposed Currency System for Academic Peer Review Payments Using the BlockChain Technology(continued)

  1. Suggest the need for a registration process for new journals to join the r-coin currency to prevent unscrupulous people from setting up publications that do not conform to the rules agreed by the community
  2. Predatory journals should be excluded from participating
  3. Transaction Mechanisms, R-Coin Exchange and Inter-Currency Exchange

The Blockchain and Kudos

  1. The Blockchain as a Distributed Digital Record
  2. Distributed system for educational records, reputation and rewards
  3. Trusted educational records combined with a system of tradeable reputation
  4. Reputation as a tradeable commodity
  5. Undertaking initial trials of a private blockchain for storing educational records, drawing also on their previous research into reputation management for educational systems

Blockchain for Open Science and Knowledge Creation

A technical solution to the reproducibility crisis

  1. ICOs for research
  2. Permissioned vs Permissionless
  3. Private vs Public
  4. Suggest Permissioned, Public Blockchains for Science
  5. Blockchain and the research cycle - Experiment->data acquisition->Data management->analysis->Data sharing->Publication/Archiving->Research evaluation->Research funding

Towards Open Science: The Case for a DecentralizedAutonomous Academic Endorsement System

  1. Propose an Academic Endorsement System (AES) with a form of currency, Academic Endorsement Points(AEP) - measure his/her impact
  2. Advantages:
    • Any kind of scientific output could be endorsed; thereby facilitating the attribution of non-traditional publications
  3. Use of DAOs
